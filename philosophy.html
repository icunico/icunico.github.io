The Era of Complexity: Hyperconnected

Do not fear, you can have everything here, right now. Indian food? Call Just Eat. Clothes from the latest runway show? Go to Amazon. Sex? Sign up on Tinder. Travel? Book on Volagratis. Fast desire, even faster gratification.

Deadlines. Doing 100 things at once, reading 100 news pieces at once, scrolling through 100 Instagram stories at once. Well done, quick, run.

You are successful as a scientist if you publish 1,248 papers a year, as an artist if you release 4,474 music albums a year, as a director if you produce 4,732 TV series a year.

And if you don't produce, you are out. "Eh, sorry, it's the system, either you adapt or you're out."

But, just to understand, what is the actual quality of what we write, watch, and listen to? What about the importance of long-term goals? Of an object built with purpose that doesn't break two seconds after purchase? Of deep connections? Of well-nurtured friendships? Of reading a text and pausing to critically reflect on its meaning? I almost miss the endless hours in high school spent paraphrasing Dante's Paradiso, or worse, translating Latin texts. Plenty of frustrations, but deep, genuine analysis, word by word.

And instead, now, everything is a race. But don't you also feel this overwhelming sense of anxiety? Always lagging behind on everything. Every time I hear the word multi-tasking, my eyes roll back. Multi-tasking what, exactly? It is already a miracle if I manage to breathe while tying my shoes.

Failing, being out—in a system like this, I don't care; in fact, I'd be a little proud of it.

The thing is, to change it, we have to keep staying in it and scrambling. Punk solutions.

But it is vital to realize that we must improve it, that we must slow it down, that short-term goals are not comparable to long-term ones, that we need to take the time to focus properly on a specific text, to reflect, to travel slowly, to know real people, to achieve a well-thought-out goal. And that having everything here, right now, is not always the best solution.
